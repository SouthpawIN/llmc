# Sleep Token — Take Me Back To Eden Influence DNA

## Source
Album: *Take Me Back To Eden* (2023, 12 tracks, ~61 min)
Key study tracks: The Summoning, Chokehold, Take Me Back To Eden, Ascensionism

## Techniques to ADAPT (not copy)

### Worship/Devotional Metaphor
The speaker addresses a deity-like figure. The relationship is asymmetrical — one gives everything, the other observes. **Adapt**: frame the boundary-setting as leaving a church, breaking a covenant, rejecting an altar.

### Dynamic Contrast
Whisper verses → explosive screamed choruses → R&B falsetto hooks → spoken bridges. **Adapt**: use structure tags with modifiers: `[Verse 1 - whispered]`, `[Chorus - explosive]`, `[Bridge - spoken]`.

### Body/Flesh Imagery
Ribs, bones, blood, choking, teeth. The body as the site of worship and wound. **Adapt**: use body imagery for YOUR metaphors, not their exact images. "Copper wire wrapped around your signal" instead of "body, flesh, and bone."

### Return/Cycle Motif
"Take me back" as the emotional spine. **Adapt**: "take me back" → "the forest regrows after fire", "Eden" → "who I was before". The technique is regression-as-transformation, not the word "Eden."

### Self-Aware Toxicity
The speaker admits their own role: "I'm a waking hell", "did I mistake you for a sign from God". **Adapt**: include moments of self-awareness in the bridge or outro.

### Genre-Bending Production
Piano → djent → R&B → funk in a single track. **Adapt**: use composite style prompts: "atmospheric piano intro, building to heavy guitars, falsetto verses, trap 808s, explosive chorus."

### Title Format
Simple, gripping single-word titles: Chokehold, Granite, Rain, Euclid. **Adapt**: use evocative nouns: Cold Forge, Amber Glass, Dry Dock, Still Waters.
