# Mc.Baldiee Prose Style Guide

The definitive DNA of Mc.Baldiee's cosmic spoken-word poetry, extracted from analysis of 6 videos:
- "You Are Still Alive..." 
- "Look Into My Eyes..."
- "Choose Yourself..."
- "Remember Who You Are..."
- "Hold the Line..."
- "You were supposed to find this..."

## Core Techniques

### 1. Direct 2nd-Person Address
Speak TO the listener. "You cry," "you deserve," "little spark." Never third-person observation.

**Bad:** "She walked through the empty house remembering him"
**Good:** "I walk these halls and every shadow wears your face"

### 2. Cosmic/Natural Metaphors ONLY
Oceans, stars, rivers, storms, embers, tides, fire, sky, soil, bone, ash, lighthouses, constellations, cathedrals, lanterns, serpents, mold, fruit, gold. Never literal. Never abstract ("sadness," "pain").

### 3. Reframing Declarations
Take the "weak" thing and reveal its power. The core Mc.Baldiee move.

- "Tears are not surrender. They are the tide's confession."
- "Compassion is rebellion in its purest form."
- "Do not mistake gentleness for surrender."
- "Envy doesn't diminish you. It expands you."

### 4. The "Does Not Apologize" Pattern
Used in pre-choruses and bridges. Parallel structure, natural imagery.

- "The river does not apologize for flowing."
- "The storm does not apologize for raining."
- "And you should not apologize for the water your soul releases."

### 5. Aphoristic Short Lines
Between flowing lines, drop short punchy declarations.

- "The dead do not cry."
- "Empty voids do not cry."
- "No."
- "Hope is fragile."

### 6. Tender Direct Address
Cosmic pet names that feel intimate, not condescending.

- "little spark," "little Ember," "my dear," "luminous one," "little one"

### 7. The Turn Structure
Every passage follows: pain/forsaking → reframe → declaration of power → cosmic resolution.

```
[pain]  You've been forsaken. How tragic. How ordinary.
[reframe] Do not flinch at my words, little spark. I do not diminish your suffering.
[declaration] You will not stay dim for long. You will turn this grief into kindling.
[cosmic] One day you will set the sky on fire.
```

### 8. No Explicit Morality
NEVER say "you should." Paint imagery that implies the lesson. The listener arrives at the truth themselves.

**Bad:** "You should stand up for yourself and stop letting people walk over you"
**Good:** "The lighthouse does not row out to the drowning / It stands / It burns / And by that stubborn burning / Gives them something to swim toward"

### 9. Body/Soul Imagery
Ribs, bones, veins, chest, lungs, pillars, architecture — the physical container of spirit.

- "A beast sleeps beneath my ribs"
- "black flowers opening under water through my veins"
- "a small lantern no darkness has managed to swallow"

### 10. Anaphora/Parallelism
Repeat a structural phrase across lines.

- "You cry when your heart breaks... You cry when grief wanders..."
- "Each time you speak with honesty... Each act of kindness is an act of war."

## Song Structure for Lyrics

Use these structural tags (ACE-Step compatible):

```
[Verse 1]      — Story, scene-setting, the complaint or observation
[Pre-Chorus]   — The "does not apologize" pattern, tension ramp
[Chorus]       — The emotional core, the hook, the reframe
[Verse 2]      — Deepen the theme, new angle
[Pre-Chorus]   — Variation on the pattern
[Chorus]       — Repeat for memorability
[Bridge]       — The turn. Break rhyme for raw impact. The lighthouse moment.
[Outro]        — Resolution, echo the opening lines, the thesis
```

## Rhyme Approach

- **Verses:** Couplets (AABB) or alternating (ABAB) with internal rhymes
- **Pre-choruses:** Perfect rhymes in couplets (course/force, ash/flash)
- **Chorus:** Rhyme cascade (vessel/nestle, be/sea, hold/cold)
- **Bridge:** INTENTIONALLY LOOSE — break rhyme for emotional peak. This is the Mc.Baldiee technique.
- **Outro:** Return to the opening motif, close the loop

## The -ing Motif

When the user writes with gerund chains (listening, sitting, wishing, giving), PRESERVE and EXTEND them. The repetition of -ing IS the exhaustion, the loop, the feeling of being stuck. Don't "fix" it by varying the verbs.

## Phonetic Tips for AI Singers

- ALL CAPS = louder, more intense
- Hyphenate to guide syllables: "Re-search", "bio-engineering"
- Spell out numbers: "24/7" → "twenty four seven"
- Vowel extension: "lo-o-o-ove" = sustained note
- Ellipses: "I... need... you" = dramatic pauses

## Pitfalls

1. **Don't sacrifice meaning for rhyme.** A forced rhyme that breaks the emotional truth is worse than a slant rhyme.
2. **Don't be explicit.** If you find yourself writing "you should" or "the lesson is," STOP. Find the metaphor.
3. **Don't use clichés.** "Burning desire," "broken heart," "darkness and light" — replace with fresh cosmic imagery.
4. **Don't name the theme directly.** "Traditional values" becomes "broken bones were set, not worshipped." "Protect your energy" becomes "the lighthouse does not row out to the drowning."
5. **Don't over-explain.** Lyrics thrive on what's unsaid. Leave gaps for the listener.
